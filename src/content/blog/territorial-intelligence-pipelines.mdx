# Building Territorial Intelligence Pipelines with R and Python

The question I get asked most often: *R or Python?* My answer: both, and here's why.

## The Two-Language Strategy

R's spatial ecosystem — `sf`, `terra`, `tidyverse` — is unmatched for exploratory spatial data analysis, visualization, and statistical modeling. Python's deep learning frameworks — PyTorch, TensorFlow, GDAL bindings — dominate the computer vision side.

The key is designing pipelines that use each language where it excels, connected through standardized intermediate formats (GeoParquet, Cloud-Optimized GeoTIFF).

## Pipeline Architecture

A territorial intelligence pipeline has five stages:

1. **Ingestion** — Satellite data from Google Earth Engine, Copernicus, or STAC catalogs
2. **Pre-processing** — Atmospheric correction, co-registration, cloud masking
3. **Feature extraction** — Spectral indices, texture features, temporal profiles
4. **Modeling** — Classification, change detection, anomaly detection
5. **Output** — Maps, dashboards, policy reports

## Lessons from 50,000 km²

After processing over 50,000 km² of multi-temporal imagery at CIT-UAI, the most important lesson: **data quality beats model complexity every time.**
